🏆 Spring 2026 Leaderboard Contest
Official Rules

Effective March 18, 2026 · Last updated March 18, 2026

1. Contest Overview

The Spring 2026 Leaderboard Contest ("Contest") is a skill-based competition hosted by Amusement Standards LLC d/b/a AmusementRating.org ("Sponsor"), with principal offices in Asbury Park, New Jersey 07712. This is a contest of skill; winners are determined by objective, verifiable performance on the AmusementRating.org platform — not by chance or random drawing.

NO PURCHASE IS NECESSARY TO ENTER OR WIN. A PURCHASE WILL NOT INCREASE YOUR CHANCES OF WINNING.

2. Eligibility

The Contest is open to legal residents of the fifty (50) United States and the District of Columbia who are eighteen (18) years of age or older at the time of entry. Employees, officers, and directors of Sponsor and their immediate family members (spouse, parent, child, sibling) and household members are not eligible. Void where prohibited by law.

3. Contest Period

The Contest begins at 12:00 AM Eastern Time on March 22, 2026 and ends at 11:59 PM Eastern Time on May 25, 2026 (Memorial Day) ("Contest Period"). Only activity logged during the Contest Period counts toward final standings.

4. How to Enter

To enter, register a free account at AmusementRating.org and participate in platform activities during the Contest Period. No purchase, payment, or subscription is required. Entry is free and open to all eligible persons. Entries are earned through:

  • Ride Logging: Verified ride logs with entrance and exit photos, earning XP per the platform's published XP schedule.
  • Facility Audits: Completion of standardized observation checklists (Roller Coaster, Flat Ride, Water Ride, Play Structure), earning XP per the platform's published XP schedule.
  • Badges and Achievements: Earning platform badges through qualifying activities, earning bonus XP per the platform's published XP schedule.

All XP values are published on the platform and apply equally to all participants. There is no mechanism by which payment or purchase increases a participant's score.

5. How Winners Are Determined

Winners are determined by total XP earned during the Contest Period as displayed on the AmusementRating.org public leaderboard. This is a skill-based ranking — participants who log more verified rides, complete more audits, and earn more badges will accumulate more XP. The three (3) participants with the highest XP totals at the close of the Contest Period will be awarded prizes as described below.

In the event of a tie, the tied participants will be ranked by: (a) number of completed audits, then (b) number of unique parks visited, then (c) earliest registration date.
